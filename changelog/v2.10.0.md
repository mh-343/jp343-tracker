## New

- **Difficulty chip on the watch page**: While watching, a small chip shows the estimated level in JLPT terms (for example "~N4" or "Mixed ~N2-N1+"). A tilde means it is our estimate; levels without a tilde come straight from the creator's own title tag like 【N3】.
- **Per-video difficulty bands**: Frequently watched videos get their own analyzed difficulty band (for example "~N4-N3") that is more specific than the channel estimate. Where no per-video band exists yet, the channel estimate is shown instead.
- **Difficulty badges in the feed**: The same levels appear on video cards on the home page, in search results and in the sidebar, so you can judge videos before opening them.
- **Show difficulty levels toggle**: On by default. The levels come from two small anonymous data files fetched from jp343.com once a day. Turning the toggle off stops all of it, including the fetches.
- **Contribute anonymous stats (optional, off by default)**: Without an account you can choose to send a weekly anonymous summary of which channels you watch (rough time buckets only, no titles, no timestamps). This improves the level estimates for everyone. Account holders already contribute through their normal sync and never send this.
